About the Project

Our project focuses on anomaly detection in the wood industry. Anomalies, such as defects or irregularities, in wood planks can have significant consequences, leading to reduced quality and increased costs.

The goal of our project is to develop a machine learning-based solution that can automatically detect anomalies in wood planks, aiding in quality control processes.

About the Model

We have developed a deep learning model based on the VGG16 architecture, pre-trained on the ImageNet dataset. The model has been fine-tuned using a custom dataset consisting of more than 5500 images of both good quality wood planks and anomalous planks.

Our model has been trained to classify images into two categories: "good" and "anomaly", facilitating the identification of defective wood planks with high accuracy.

For more details and access to the dataset used in training, you can visit our GitHub repository: Anomaly Detection GitHub Repository.
